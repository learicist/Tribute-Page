Thales of Miletus

Who was he?

  • We know very little about the precise dates and time in which Thales of Miletus lived.
  • He is widely considered to be the first Western Philosopher/Scientist.
  • With an estimated lifespan between the years 624-546 BC, Thales is now valued for his contributions to thought more highly than the details of his mortal existence.
  • As the first thinker in the Western tradition to break from the use of mythology, pantheons and the supernatural to answer metaphysical questions, Thales now serves as a beacon pointing us to the very heart of the Philosophical Endeavor. The pursuit of wisdom and truth.
What were his views?
Water as the foundational element of the Universe Thales was a Polymath, and practiced in fields ranging from Engineering to Astronomy.
Thales' Cosmology comes to us from Aristotle, who tells us of how Thales held water to be the literal be-all-and-end-all of nature. This is quite an impressive leap from the previous canon of human thought on the subject, even if today it seems easy to disprove. Called the 'first Greek Mathemetician', Thales is credited with being the first to bisect the circle. He was also proficient in Politics, Business and Geology.
